Koshary

Koshary is an iconic Egyptian street food dish that combines layers of flavors and textures into a satisfying meal. This vegetarian dish offers a harmonious blend of flavors and textures, showcasing the essence of Egyptian cuisine. Koshary is a comforting and budget-friendly meal enjoyed by many for its satisfying taste and simplicity, making it a favorite choice among locals and visitors alike.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

40 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

In a large pot, separately cook the basmati rice and brown lentils according to package instructions until tender. Drain any excess water and set aside.

step 2

In a medium pot, cook the elbow macaroni or short pasta in salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and set aside.

step 3

In a saucepan, heat a bit of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and ground cumin, and sauté for a minute until fragrant. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and season with salt. Simmer the sauce for about 10-15 minutes until it thickens slightly. Stir in the white vinegar, then remove from heat.

step 4

In a skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add thinly sliced onions and fry until golden brown and crispy. Remove the onions from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.

step 5

To serve, layer the cooked rice, lentils, and pasta in individual bowls or plates. Spoon the tomato sauce generously over the top. Garnish with crispy fried onions and optional chopped fresh parsley. Serve hot with additional hot sauce on the side if desired.

ingredients

1 cup basmati rice

1 cup brown lentils

1 cup elbow macaroni or short pasta

1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes

4 cloves garlic, minced

1 tablespoon ground cumin

1 tablespoon white vinegar

Salt, to taste

Vegetable oil, for frying

2 large onions, thinly sliced

Optional toppings: chopped fresh parsley, hot sauce

tools

Large pot for cooking rice and lentils

Medium pot for cooking pasta

Skillet for frying onions

Mixing spoon

Strainer or colander


Garlic Ranch Mashed Potatoes

Garlic Ranch Mashed Potatoes are a delicious twist on classic mashed potatoes, infused with the flavors of garlic and ranch seasoning. This creamy side dish combines fluffy mashed potatoes with minced garlic, butter, milk, and a packet of ranch seasoning mix for a savory and tangy taste. The addition of garlic adds depth and richness, while the ranch seasoning brings a unique herbaceous flavor profile. Garlic Ranch Mashed Potatoes are a comforting and versatile accompaniment to any meal, perfect for holiday gatherings or weeknight dinners. They offer a delightful balance of creamy texture and bold flavors that will satisfy potato lovers everywhere.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

20 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Cook 2 pounds of peeled and chopped potatoes in boiling water until tender, about 15-20 minutes.

step 2

In a saucepan, melt 4 tablespoons of butter with 4 minced garlic cloves over low heat for 1-2 minutes.

step 3

Drain the cooked potatoes and return them to the pot. Mash them until smooth.

step 4

Pour the garlic butter over the mashed potatoes. Sprinkle in 1 packet of ranch seasoning mix and gradually mix in 1/2 cup of milk or cream until creamy.

step 5

Season with salt and pepper to taste. Transfer to a serving bowl and garnish with chopped fresh herbs if desired.

ingredients

2 pounds potatoes (such as russet or Yukon gold), peeled and cut into chunks

4 cloves garlic, minced

4 tablespoons unsalted butter

1/2 cup milk or cream

1 packet (1 ounce) ranch seasoning mix

Salt and pepper, to taste

Chopped fresh chives or parsley, for garnish (optional)

tools

Large pot for boiling potatoes

Colander

Potato masher or handheld mixer

Saucepan

Wooden spoon or spatula


Forest Wassail Drink

Wassail is an ancient brew for dreaming and protection, celebration and healing, and for welcoming in a new season and year of potential. Wassail is a celebratory drink with Pagan roots that pre-dates christianity in its origin with stories that stretch across the British Isles. In the Norse tradition, Wassail would have been called "Ves Heil", and in English "was hál", meaning "good health" or "good fortune".

prep time

10 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

8-10 servings

instructions

step 1

In a large pot or slow cooker, combine the apple cider, cranberry juice, and orange juice.

step 2

Stir in the brown sugar until dissolved.

step 3

Add the whole cloves and cinnamon sticks to the pot.

step 4

Place the orange and lemon slices into the mixture.

step 5

Heat the wassail over medium-low heat on the stovetop or set the slow cooker to low.

step 6

Let the wassail simmer gently for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

step 7

Serve the Forest Wassail hot in mugs, making sure to include a slice of orange or lemon in each serving.

ingredients

1 gallon (about 16 cups) apple cider or apple juice

2 cups cranberry juice

1 cup orange juice

Sprinkling of Nutmeg

3 Tablespoons of Dried Hawthorn Berries

1/2 cup brown sugar

1 teaspoon whole cloves

3 cinnamon sticks

1 orange, sliced

1/4 Cup Cranberries

1 lemon, sliced

tools

Large pot or slow cooker

Stirring spoon or ladle

Knife and cutting board

Serving mugs

  
  

Classic Tomato Bruschetta

Classic Tomato Bruschetta is a classic Italian appetizer featuring diced tomatoes, fresh basil, garlic, and olive oil piled onto toasted bread. This dish captures the essence of Mediterranean flavors with its simple yet vibrant combination of ingredients. Each bite offers a burst of juicy tomatoes, aromatic basil, and a hint of garlic, all balanced on crispy bread. Tomato bruschetta is a beloved starter that highlights the freshness of seasonal produce and is perfect for any occasion.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

5 min.

serving

4-6 servings

instructions

step 1

Dice the ripe tomatoes and finely chop the fresh basil leaves. Place them in a mixing bowl.

step 2

Mince the garlic cloves and add them to the bowl with the tomatoes and basil. Drizzle with extra-virgin olive oil.

step 3

Season the tomato mixture with salt and pepper to taste. Gently mix everything together until well combined. Set aside to marinate for a few minutes.

step 4

Slice the baguette or Italian bread into 1/2-inch thick slices. Arrange the slices on a baking sheet and toast them in the oven or toaster oven until golden brown and crisp.

step 5

Once the bread slices are toasted, spoon the tomato mixture generously onto each slice of bread, ensuring to include some of the juices.

step 6

Drizzle the assembled bruschetta with balsamic glaze or a vinegar reduction for added flavor and presentation.

step 7

Arrange the prepared Classic Tomato Bruschetta on a serving platter and serve immediately as a delicious appetizer or starter.

ingredients

4-5 ripe tomatoes, diced

1-2 cloves garlic, minced

8-10 fresh basil leaves, finely chopped

2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil

Salt and pepper, to taste

1 baguette or Italian bread, sliced

Optional: Balsamic glaze or vinegar reduction for drizzling

tools

Cutting board

Knife

Mixing bowl

Spoon or spatula

Baking sheet or toaster oven

  
  

Vegan Chickpea and Potato Curry

Vegan Chickpea and Potato Curry is a hearty and flavorful dish featuring tender potatoes, protein-packed chickpeas, and aromatic spices in a creamy coconut milk sauce. This vegan curry is seasoned with garlic, ginger, and curry powder for a delicious blend of flavors. It's a satisfying meal that's completely plant-based and perfect served over rice or with naan bread. Enjoy this comforting dish as a nourishing option for dinner!

prep time

15 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, and ginger. Sauté for 5 minutes until onion softens.

step 2

Add bell peppers, potato, and chickpeas. Cook for 5 minutes until vegetables start to soften.

step 3

Stir in diced tomatoes (with juices) and coconut milk. Add curry powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

step 4

Simmer uncovered for 20-25 minutes until potatoes are tender and curry thickens, stirring occasionally.

step 5

Taste and adjust seasoning as needed with salt and pepper.

step 6

Serve hot over cooked rice or with naan bread.

step 7

Garnish with chopped cilantro before serving.

ingredients

1 tablespoon coconut oil or vegetable oil

1 onion, diced

3 cloves garlic, minced

1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger

2 bell peppers (any color), diced

1 large potato, peeled and diced

1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed

1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes

1 can (14 oz) coconut milk

1 tablespoon curry powder

Salt and pepper, to taste

Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Cooked rice or naan bread, for serving

tools

Large skillet or pot

Wooden spoon or spatula

Cutting board and knife

Grater (for ginger)

Can opener

Measuring spoons

Serving bowls and utensils
