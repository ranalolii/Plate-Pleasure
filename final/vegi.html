Mushroom Risotto

Mushroom risotto is a classic Italian dish made with Arborio rice cooked in a flavorful broth until creamy and tender. It features earthy mushrooms such as cremini or porcini, sautéed with onions, garlic, and thyme to enhance the savory flavors. The rice is slowly cooked and stirred while adding warm broth gradually until it reaches a rich, creamy consistency. Finished with Parmesan cheese and a knob of butter, mushroom risotto is a comforting and elegant dish, perfect for showcasing the rich umami flavors of mushrooms.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Heat the vegetable broth in a saucepan and keep it warm on low heat

step 2

In a large skillet, heat olive oil and butter over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, sauté until softened

step 3

Add the Arborio rice to the skillet and cook for 1-2 minutes until slightly toasted.

step 4

Pour in the white wine (if using) and stir until absorbed.

step 5

Begin adding the warm vegetable broth to the rice, one ladleful at a time, stirring frequently until absorbed.

step 6

Continue adding broth and stirring until the rice is creamy and cooked al dente (about 20-25 minutes).

step 7

Stir in the sliced mushrooms during the last 5 minutes of cooking.

step 8

Remove from heat, stir in grated Parmesan cheese, season with salt and pepper.

step 9

Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley

ingredients

Arborio rice (1 cup)

Mushrooms (sliced, 8 oz)

Vegetable broth (4 cups)

Onion (1, finely chopped)

Garlic cloves (2, minced)

Parmesan cheese (1/2 cup, grated)

Butter (2 tbsp)

Olive oil (2 tbsp)

Salt and pepper (to taste)

Fresh parsley (chopped, for garnish)

tools

Large skillet or saucepan

Wooden spoon

Ladle

Grater

Cutting board and knife


Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Shells

Spinach and ricotta stuffed shells are a delightful Italian-American pasta dish. Jumbo pasta shells are filled with a mixture of creamy ricotta cheese, chopped spinach, garlic, and Parmesan cheese. The shells are then arranged in a baking dish, topped with marinara sauce, and baked until bubbly and golden. This dish is comforting, cheesy, and packed with flavors from the spinach and ricotta mixture, making it a satisfying meal that's perfect for gatherings or family dinners.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

6 servings

instructions

step 1

Cook pasta shells according to package instructions. Drain and rinse under cold water.

step 2

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).

step 3

In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, chopped spinach, shredded mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.

step 4

Stuff each cooked shell with the ricotta-spinach mixture.

step 5

Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.

step 6

Arrange stuffed shells in the dish, then cover with remaining marinara sauce.

step 7

Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.

step 8

Remove foil and bake for an additional 5 minutes until bubbly and golden on top.

step 9

Serve hot with extra Parmesan cheese if desired.

ingredients

Jumbo pasta shells (1 box)

Ricotta cheese (15 oz)

Frozen chopped spinach (10 oz, thawed and drained)

Mozzarella cheese (1 cup, shredded)

Parmesan cheese (1/2 cup, grated)

Egg (1)

Butter (2 tbsp)

Garlic powder (1 tsp)

Salt and pepper (to taste)

Marinara sauce (2 cups)

tools

Large pot for boiling water

Mixing bowl

Baking dish

Aluminum foil


Chickpea and Vegetable Curry

Chickpea and vegetable curry is a vibrant and nourishing dish with origins in Indian cuisine. It features a medley of colorful vegetables such as bell peppers, carrots, and peas, simmered in a fragrant curry sauce made from coconut milk, curry spices, and ginger-garlic paste. Tender chickpeas add protein and substance to the curry. This dish is best served over steamed rice or with naan bread, garnished with fresh cilantro for a burst of freshness.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

25 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Heat olive oil and sauté onions, garlic, and ginger until translucent.

step 2

Stir in curry powder briefly.

step 3

Add vegetables, chickpeas, coconut milk, and vegetable broth; season.

step 4

Simmer uncovered for 15-20 minutes until thickened.

step 5

Adjust seasoning and serve over rice or with naan.

step 6

Garnish with fresh cilantro.

ingredients

Chickpeas (2 cans, drained and rinsed)

Mixed vegetables (e.g., bell peppers, cauliflower, carrots) (3 cups, chopped)

Onion (1, chopped)

Garlic cloves (2, minced)

Ginger (1-inch piece, minced)

Curry powder (2 tbsp)

Coconut milk (1 can)

Vegetable broth (1 cup)

Olive oil (2 tbsp)

Salt and pepper (to taste)

Fresh cilantro (chopped, for garnish)

Cooked rice or naan (for serving)

tools

Large skillet or pot

Wooden spoon

Knife and cutting board

  
  

Caprese Salad with Balsamic Glaze

A Caprese salad with balsamic glaze is a simple yet elegant dish showcasing the flavors of fresh tomatoes, creamy mozzarella cheese, and aromatic basil. The salad is assembled by arranging slices of ripe tomatoes and mozzarella on a plate, alternating with basil leaves. It's then drizzled with a sweet and tangy balsamic glaze, made by reducing balsamic vinegar to a syrupy consistency. This salad is a celebration of summer produce and Italian flavors, perfect as a starter or side dish to complement any meal.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

5 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Arrange sliced tomatoes and mozzarella alternately on a serving platter.

step 2

Tuck fresh basil leaves between the tomato and mozzarella slices.

step 3

Drizzle with balsamic glaze and extra virgin olive oil.

step 4

Season with salt and pepper to taste.

step 5

Serve immediately as a refreshing appetizer or side dish.

ingredients

Fresh ripe tomatoes (4, sliced)

Fresh mozzarella cheese (8 oz, sliced)

Fresh basil leaves (1 bunch)

Balsamic glaze (store-bought or homemade)

Extra virgin olive oil (2 tbsp)

Salt and pepper (to taste)

tools

Serving platter

Sharp knife

Small bowl for mixing

  
  

Ratatouille Recipe

Ratatouille is a classic French vegetable stew that embodies the flavors of Provence. It features a medley of colorful vegetables such as eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, and tomatoes, all simmered together with garlic, onions, and herbs like thyme and basil. The dish is traditionally cooked slowly to allow the flavors to meld, resulting in a rich and aromatic stew. Ratatouille is versatile and can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature, served as a side dish, over pasta, or as a main course with crusty bread. It's a comforting and wholesome dish that showcases the bounty of summer vegetables in a delicious and rustic preparation.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

45 min.

serving

4-6 servings

instructions

step 1

Dice the eggplant, zucchini, red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, and onion into uniform-sized pieces.

step 2

Heat olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add diced onions and minced garlic, and sauté for 2-3 minutes until softened and fragrant.

step 3

Add the diced eggplant, zucchini, and bell peppers to the skillet. Stir well to combine with the onions and garlic. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.

step 4

Stir in the diced tomatoes (or canned tomatoes) and tomato paste. Season with dried thyme, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix everything together thoroughly.

step 5

Reduce the heat to low and cover the skillet. Let the ratatouille simmer for 25-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until all the vegetables are tender and the flavors have melded together.

step 6

Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. Add more salt and pepper as desired. Remove from heat.

step 7

Sprinkle chopped fresh basil over the ratatouille just before serving. Ratatouille can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature. Serve as a side dish, over pasta, or as a main course with crusty bread.

ingredients

1 medium eggplant, diced

2 medium zucchini, diced

1 red bell pepper, diced

1 yellow bell pepper, diced

1 onion, diced

3 cloves garlic, minced

4 ripe tomatoes, diced (or 1 can of diced tomatoes)

2 tablespoons tomato paste

2 tablespoons olive oil

1 teaspoon dried thyme

1 teaspoon dried oregano

Salt and pepper, to taste

Fresh basil leaves, chopped (for garnish)

tools

Large skillet or Dutch oven

Knife

Cutting board

Wooden spoon or spatula
